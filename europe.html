As a mortal, Ọṣun was the beautiful third wife of the Emperor Ṣàngó of the Oyo kingdom. As an Òrìṣà, she is the embodiment of love, femininity, beauty, and fertility. She is the spirit of sweet waters and the protective deity of the River Ọṣun in Nigeria. When the Supreme Creator Olódùmarè withheld rain from the world after the Òrìṣà offended them, it was Ọṣun, the youngest of the Orisha, who transformed into a peacock and flew to heaven to commune with the Supreme Creator.

By the time she arrived, her feathers had molted from the sun and she was no longer a beautiful peacock, but a vulture. Olódùmarè was moved by her sacrifice and persistence and agreed to return the rains to the earth, even making Ọṣun their emissary. To this day, those who wish to speak with Olódùmarè must go through Ọṣun to do so.

She is the third and favorite wife of Ṣàngó and the mother of Ibejí, the sacred twins. Though she is known as one of the most powerful

Òrìṣàs and is usually benevolent, Ọṣun still demonstrates human emotions like spite and and envy. In a fit of jealousy, she convinced Ṣàngó's first wife, Ọbà, to cut off her ear so that she would no longer rival Ọṣun's beauty. However, even with these tendencies, she is often seen as a protector, nurture, and one who gives life.
